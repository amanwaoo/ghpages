About Me!

Hi, I'm Aman Waoo, an enthusiastic graduate student pursuing a Master's in Electrical and Computer Engineering at North Carolina State University. With a current GPA of 4.0, I'm on track to graduate in May 2024.

My academic journey includes a Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering from Maulana Azad National Institute of Technology (NIT-Bhopal). During undergrad, I built a strong foundation in programming with a focus on C++, Python, and MATLAB, along with exploring introductory courses in AI/ML.

At NCSU, my coursework spans subjects like computer vision, imaging systems, pattern recognition, neural networks and deep learning, advanced machine learning, providing a comprehensive understanding of the latest technologies in the field.

Proficient in Python, SQL, C++, RStudio, and MATLAB, I've honed my technical skills throughout my professional journey. Well-versed in various Python frameworks and libraries relevant to the CV/ML stack, I bring a strong technical foundation to the table.

Beyond the classroom, my passion for computer vision and data science is evident in research and internships. As a ML Student Intern at Sozzani Lab NCSU, I contribute to development of learning models predicting transcriptional Activation Domains (ADs) in Arabidopsis gene regulatory networks, surpassing current benchmarks. My role also involves conducting Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) using Python and SQL.

In the summer of '23, I was a Data Science Intern at Schlumberger (SLB) in Houston. My primary focus during this period was working in Prognostics Health Monitoring (PHM) for preventive maintenance, utilizing advanced statistical techniques to analyze intricate datasets obtained from electronic sensor boards in the oil drilling process. I also worked on balancing of datasets through the implementation of oversampling techniques and GAN networks. This effort played a pivotal role in the development of intelligent triggers for further developed ML-models, resulting in exceptional performance.
